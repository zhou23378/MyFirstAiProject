# Interview Guide Template

## Before the Interview

- Define the ONE thing you want to learn (not five things)
- Write your 3 scariest assumptions about the problem
- Prepare 5-7 questions max — you won't get through more in 30 minutes

## Question Template

### Opening (2 min)
"Thanks for taking the time. I'm trying to understand how people [do X]. There are no right answers — I'm learning."

### The Five Questions (15-20 min)

1. "What's the hardest part about [doing X]?"
2. "Can you tell me about the last time that happened?"
3. "Why was that hard?"
4. "What have you done to try to solve it?"
5. "What don't you love about those solutions?"

### Digging Deeper (5-10 min)

- "How often does this come up?"
- "Can you show me how you handle it today?"
- "Have you looked for or paid for a solution?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?" (Use sparingly — hypothetical, but useful for framing)

### Closing (2-3 min)

- "Who else deals with this problem that I should talk to?"
- "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"

## Bad vs Good Questions

| Bad (Hypothetical/Leading) | Good (Behavioral/Specific) |
|---|---|
| "Would you use a tool that does X?" | "How do you handle X today?" |
| "How much would you pay for this?" | "Have you spent money trying to solve this?" |
| "Don't you think X is a problem?" | "Tell me about the last time X happened." |
| "Would this feature be useful?" | "What's the most annoying part of your current workflow?" |
| "Do you like this idea?" | "What have you tried so far?" |

## Commitment Currencies (Signal Strength)

After the interview, assess what they gave you. Ordered from weakest to strongest:

1. **Time** — They agreed to talk (weak — everyone's polite)
2. **Reputation** — They introduced you to someone ("Talk to my colleague Sarah")
3. **Access** — They shared data, screenshots, or let you observe their workflow
4. **Money** — They pre-ordered, paid for a pilot, or offered budget
5. **Behavior change** — They started using your prototype or changed their workflow

If all you got was "sounds great, keep me posted" — you learned nothing. Push for advancement.
